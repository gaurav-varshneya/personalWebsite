About Me

I’m currently a second-year student in the process of completing a Combined Degree in Business and Computer Science at the University of British Columbia.

If I had my own talk show it would definitely be all about technology and cars. The two things I’ve had a passion for as long as I can remember. Either it was playing with matchbox cars or using and figuring out how all the electronics worked for hours on end. As I started getting older I would tinker around with electronics, opening them up and putting them back together, modifying my game consoles, and eventually building my computer; which was like one large Lego set.

In grade 8 I remember begging my mom to let me take a web development class which taught basic HTML alongside with basketball during the summer. She agreed, and that’s where I like to think my interest for coding really took off. In grade 11 and 12 I started learning about Visual Basic, Javascript, more HTML, PHP, and a bit of Java. I also completed additional work experience within those two years at The Leukemia and Lymphoma Society of Canada working with excel and at The B.C Technology for Learning Society stripping down old computers, and reusing those parts to create computers for underprivileged kids.
